# Job Lifecycle

Jobs connect sold work to production, field execution, billing, and profitability.

## Initial Lifecycle

1. Planning - approved work is being scoped for production.
2. Scheduled - crew, dates, materials, and dependencies are assigned.
3. In progress - work is actively being performed.
4. On hold - blocked by weather, client, materials, payment, or internal review.
5. Complete - field work is done and ready for closeout review.
6. Closed - billing, files, and profitability review are complete.

## Job Context

Each job should preserve customer, site, manager, contract value, estimated cost, actual cost, dates, files, invoices, and audit history.

## Closeout

Closeout should verify photos, punch list completion, final invoice readiness, payment status, and margin variance before closing the job.
