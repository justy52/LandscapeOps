# Reporting

Reporting should help owners understand sales health, production pressure, billing performance, and profitability.

## Initial Reporting Areas

- Lead source conversion and pipeline value.
- Estimate win rate and average approval time.
- Active jobs by status and manager.
- Schedule workload and route pressure.
- Invoiced revenue, overdue AR, and payment status.
- Estimated vs actual job cost.
- Gross margin and margin variance.

## Rules

Reports must be org-scoped, reproducible, and explicit about date ranges. Financial reports should use cents-based values and avoid mixing draft, approved, invoiced, and paid totals without labels.

## UX Direction

Reporting should be executive-grade but practical: strong summaries first, drill-down tables second, and clear status colors for risk.
